Smart Routing: How Arken Finds the Best Swap Execution on Every Trade

Arken's smart routing engine is the core of its value proposition — the algorithm that determines the optimal path for every swap across all available DEX liquidity on the selected chain.

Routing typeHow it worksWhen Arken uses it
Direct single-DEX route Entire swap executed on the single DEX with the best price Sufficient liquidity on one DEX, no benefit from splitting
Split route Swap divided across 2+ DEXs (e.g. 60% PancakeSwap + 40% Biswap) to reduce price impact Large trades where a single pool would suffer high slippage
Multi-hop route Swap routed through an intermediate token (A→B→C) when direct pair has insufficient depth Low-liquidity direct pairs where an indirect path offers better output
Composite route Combination of splits and hops across multiple DEXs in a single transaction Complex swaps for less common token pairs requiring optimal path construction
Net output is what matters: Arken's routing accounts for both the swap output and the estimated gas cost of each route — a split across three DEXs that extracts 0.5% more tokens but costs $2 more in gas may be worse than a direct swap on Ethereum mainnet. On low-gas chains (BNB Chain, Avalanche), gas is minimal relative to swap output, so routing can optimise almost entirely on price improvement.

DEX Limit Orders on Arken: Automated Execution Without Custody

Arken's DEX limit order system allows traders to set a target price for a swap and have Arken automatically execute when the market reaches that price — without ever taking custody of the trader's tokens.

How Arken limit orders work

You sign a limit order specifying: token pair, exact amount to sell, minimum amount to receive, and order expiry. Your tokens remain in your wallet until execution — Arken's keeper network monitors prices and executes the trade when the market reaches your target, at which point your tokens move directly from your wallet to the output in a single transaction.

Use cases for DEX limit orders

Buy the dip: set a limit buy for BNB at a target price below market without watching charts. Take profit: set a limit sell at your target exit price without timing the market manually. Stop-loss simulation: set a sell order at a price below current to limit downside automatically. All without leaving funds on a centralised exchange.

Not a traditional order book: Arken's limit orders are not matched peer-to-peer like a centralised exchange order book. They execute when a keeper detects that the market price (via a DEX) satisfies your limit — meaning execution timing depends on keeper activity and DEX price movement. In fast-moving markets, execution may occur at or near your target price but with some deviation due to slippage at execution time.

How to Swap on Arken Finance: Step-by-Step Tutorial

  1. Navigate to the official Arken Finance app — use a bookmarked URL (arken.finance). Verify the domain before connecting your wallet — DEX aggregator phishing sites are common.
  2. Connect your wallet — MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Coinbase Wallet, or any EVM-compatible wallet. Switch to your desired chain before connecting — Arken will match your wallet's active network.
  3. Select the swap type — choose between "Swap" (market order at best available price) or "Limit Order" (execute at your specified target price).
  4. Select input and output tokens — use the token search or paste a verified contract address for unlisted tokens. For unfamiliar tokens, check Arken's built-in security scan result before proceeding.
  5. Enter the amount and review the route — Arken displays the routing breakdown: which DEXs are used, percentage splits, and estimated output. Higher price impact warnings require acknowledgment before proceeding.
  6. Review slippage and gas settings — default slippage is usually 0.5% for major pairs. Adjust for tokens with high volatility or transaction taxes. Review the gas estimate before confirming.
  7. Approve token (first time) — approve Arken's router contract to interact with your input token. Use exact-amount approval where possible rather than unlimited.
  8. Confirm the swap — sign the transaction. On BNB Chain and Avalanche, confirmation is near-instant. Track the transaction on BscScan or Snowtrace to verify execution.
For limit orders: After selecting "Limit Order", set your target price (the price at which you want to buy or sell), review the minimum received amount, set an expiry, and sign the limit order. Your tokens remain in your wallet. Monitor open orders in the Arken interface and cancel before expiry if no longer needed.

Best Practices for Arken Finance Traders

  • Always verify contract addresses for new tokens before trading — especially tokens surfaced in the "New Listings" or "Trending" sections. Arken's in-app security scan is a first filter; use TokenSniffer and GoPlus for a second opinion on unfamiliar tokens.
  • Use exact-amount token approvals rather than unlimited — when Arken asks to approve a token, approve only the amount of your current swap. Reduce accumulated unlimited approvals via Revoke.cash.
  • Compare Arken's quote with a direct DEX quote for large trades — while Arken's routing almost always improves output for significant trade sizes, verifying on the primary DEX occasionally confirms the improvement is real and not an edge case.
  • For limit orders: set realistic expiry times — a limit order that expires before market conditions reach your target is wasted. Set expiry conservatively (3–7 days for most setups) rather than very short windows unless the strategy is specifically time-sensitive.
  • Keep a gas buffer in native chain tokens — BSC transactions require BNB, Avalanche requires AVAX. Swapping 100% of your native token to another asset leaves you unable to execute future transactions or cancel limit orders.
  • Review the routing breakdown for large swaps — Arken shows which DEXs and percentages are used. If routing through a DEX you're unfamiliar with, verify that DEX is legitimate on BscScan before confirming.
  • Bookmark arken.finance and verify the domain every session — aggregator phishing is common. Never navigate to Arken via search ads or links from DMs.

Troubleshooting Arken Finance: Failed Swaps, Limit Orders, and Missing Tokens

"My swap transaction failed on Arken"

  • Check the failed transaction on the chain's block explorer (BscScan for BSC, Snowtrace for Avalanche) — the revert reason indicates the specific failure. Common causes: slippage exceeded (price moved between quote and execution), token transaction tax exceeding slippage tolerance, or insufficient gas limit.
  • For tokens with taxes (buy/sell tax), increase your slippage to at least tax % + 1–2% buffer. If the token charges a 5% sell tax, set slippage to at least 7% for reliable execution.
  • If the routing used multiple DEXs and one of them failed mid-route, the whole transaction reverts — your tokens are safe. Try again with a fresh quote, which may route differently.

"My limit order hasn't filled despite reaching my price"

  • Keeper execution has a small delay — if the market touched your limit price briefly and rebounded, the order may not have been detected and executed within that window. Keepers require sufficient gas incentive and market stability to fill orders reliably.
  • Check that your limit order hasn't expired — if the expiry passed without fill, the order is cancelled and your tokens are unlocked. Create a new order with an updated target if still relevant.
  • Very small limit orders may be skipped by keepers if the execution fee exceeds the economic benefit. Ensure your limit order size is meaningful relative to the chain's current gas costs.

"A token I want to trade doesn't appear in Arken's search"

  • Paste the token's contract address directly into the Arken token search field — this imports any BEP-20/ERC-20 token regardless of whether it's indexed in Arken's token list.
  • Verify the contract address from the token's official sources (project website, verified BscScan/Snowtrace page) before pasting — never use contract addresses from social media or DMs.
Chain explorer is ground truth: For any Arken transaction outcome, the chain's native block explorer (BscScan, Snowtrace, PolygonScan) provides the authoritative record. The Arken UI may occasionally lag on-chain state — check the explorer if there's any ambiguity about whether a transaction succeeded.

Arken Finance: Frequently Asked Questions

Arken Finance is a multi-chain DEX aggregator — it doesn't operate its own liquidity pools but instead routes trades across multiple DEXs (PancakeSwap, Trader Joe, QuickSwap, etc.) to find the best price for any swap. Unlike a regular DEX where you trade against one pool, Arken may split your trade across three DEXs simultaneously to reduce price impact and maximise your output. It also adds DEX-native limit orders and token discovery analytics not available on standalone DEXs.

Arken's limit orders are non-custodial — your tokens never leave your wallet until the order executes. You sign a cryptographic order specifying the token pair, sell amount, minimum receive amount, and expiry. Arken's keeper network monitors DEX prices and automatically executes the swap directly from your wallet when the market reaches your target. If the order expires unfilled, nothing happens — your tokens remain accessible throughout. You can cancel at any time with a small gas fee.

Arken supports BNB Smart Chain, Avalanche C-Chain, Polygon, Fantom, Arbitrum One, Optimism, Cronos, and Ethereum — with ongoing expansion. BNB Chain and Avalanche have the deepest DEX integrations as Arken's primary markets. Connect your wallet on any supported network and Arken automatically switches to aggregating liquidity for that chain. Check the official Arken documentation for the current full list of supported chains and DEXs.

Arken's built-in token explorer shows: trending tokens by volume and trade count (1h/6h/24h windows), new token listings with initial liquidity events, top gainers and losers, real-time price charts from DEX data, liquidity depth for any pair, and basic security flags (honeypot detection, high tax warning, unverified contract). This eliminates the need to switch to separate platforms like DexTools or GeckoTerminal for basic token research before trading.

Arken charges a small protocol fee on swaps — typically a fraction of a percent added to the underlying DEX fee. For limit orders, a keeper execution fee is charged at fill time to incentivise the keeper network. ARKEN token holders may qualify for reduced fees. The "You receive" amount shown in Arken's swap interface accounts for all fees (Arken protocol fee + underlying DEX fee) — use this net figure to compare against direct DEX quotes. Check current fee rates in the official Arken documentation.

For any swap, Arken evaluates direct routes, multi-hop routes (A→B→C), and split routes (partial execution across multiple DEXs) and finds the combination that maximises your output after all fees. For a large BNB→TOKEN swap, Arken might route 60% through PancakeSwap and 40% through Biswap — reducing price impact on each individual pool. On low-gas chains like BSC and Avalanche, the gas overhead of multi-DEX routing is minimal relative to the price improvement for trades above roughly $500.

Arken's router contracts are audited and the protocol has operated without a major exploit. The main risks for large trades are: smart-contract risk in Arken's router (audited but not zero), the underlying DEX risk (routes through third-party DEXs), and token-specific risks (the token itself may be a scam regardless of the aggregator). For very large trades, compare Arken's quote against direct DEX execution and verify routing quality. Use exact-amount approvals rather than unlimited to limit exposure, and revoke stale approvals after large trading sessions.

Both are DEX aggregators, but Arken has deeper BNB Chain DEX integrations — more integrated DEXs means more routing options and better price improvement on BSC-native token pairs. 1inch's Ethereum routing is more mature, but for BSC-specific trading (especially newer or smaller-cap BSC tokens), Arken's routing quality and token discovery tools are typically superior. For large swaps on major pairs (BNB/USDT, BNB/BUSD), comparing quotes from both before executing is a sensible approach to verify best execution.
